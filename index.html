Motion Simulating Robot Through Hand Gestures

Project Brief:

This project focuses on developing a real-time IoT-based monitoring system that allows humans to interact with robots through hand gestures, offering substantial assistance to individuals with mobility challenges. By employing advanced gesture recognition technology, the system effectively bridges the gap between the physical and digital worlds, providing an intuitive means of control that does not require manual operation. The core of the system lies in its ability to recognize gestures using an Accelerometer and Bluetooth Module, enabling users to control the robot with simple hand movements. This interaction is made possible by the integration of the NodeMCU microcontroller, which ensures accurate and real-time communication between the gestures and the robot, resulting in a responsive and user-friendly experience. To further enhance functionality, the system is equipped with an IP Camera, allowing users to monitor the robot's movements and surroundings in real-time. Additionally, the inclusion of an Ultrasonic Sensor for automatic obstacle detection significantly improves safety by preventing potential hazards during the robot's operation. The robot's mobility is supported by a DC Motor and Battery, ensuring continuous and smooth movement. Overall, this project demonstrates the potential of IoT and gesture recognition technologies in improving human-robot interaction, particularly for individuals with mobility impairments. The system offers an efficient, accessible, and user-friendly solution that enhances the quality of life for those who face challenges with traditional manual controls.
